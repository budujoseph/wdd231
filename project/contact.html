The History of PEP Sports Foundation

The PEP Sports Foundation, known today as a global leader in athletic development and sports innovation, had humble beginnings in the small town of Brighton, England, in 1985. Founded by former professional footballer Peter E. Pearson, the foundation started as a local initiative aimed at providing young athletes with access to proper training and facilities. Peter Pearson, affectionately known as “Coach Pete,” envisioned a world where every child, regardless of socioeconomic background, could pursue their dreams in sports. With a modest initial funding from local businesses and personal savings, Pearson opened the first PEP Training Center in an abandoned warehouse, transforming it into a vibrant hub for young athletes. By the early 1990s, PEP Sports had gained national recognition for its holistic approach to athlete development, combining physical training with mental conditioning and academic support. This unique methodology attracted top talent and soon led to partnerships with renowned sports organizations. In 2001, PEP Sports Foundation expanded internationally, establishing training centers in Australia, South Africa, and the United States. Its innovative programs, such as the PEP Elite Academy and the PEP Community Outreach Initiative, have since impacted thousands of athletes worldwide. Today, the PEP Sports Foundation continues to thrive, upholding Pearson’s legacy by fostering talent, promoting sportsmanship, and building future champions across the globe.
